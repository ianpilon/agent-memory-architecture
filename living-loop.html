Mental model / our version

Memory as a living loop, not geology

Google modeled agent memory as sediment settling under gravity into permanent bedrock. That is a picture of storage. Here is the same substrate drawn as what our thesis actually claims it is: a closed loop that keeps reconstructing a self.

one continuous cycle · the self sits at the center and is remade by every pass
1 · encode
The stream is gated by goals and salience before anything is stored. Nothing settles blind.
2 · consolidate
A background process compresses episodes into a revisable web. It can be rewritten and forgotten.
3 · reconstruct
On the way out, memory is a seed regrown in context through the bowtie, never fetched raw.
4 · act & return
Action changes the world, the world feeds the stream, and the self re-gates the next pass.
The elements

Four elements and one transform, arranged as a loop

The Stream
world in
Raw prompts, tool outputs, events. Transient and unstructured. The one place we fully agree with Google.
Working-Self Gate
goal + salience
A filter every item crosses in both directions: what gets encoded, and what is allowed to surface, is decided by current goals and emotional weight. Geology has no gate; sediment settles goal-blind.
Revisable Substrate
knowledge web
Consolidated, interconnected, and rewritable. Facts get superseded, the self can forget. Google's "permanent bedrock" bakes in stale knowledge and, by our own conservatism principle, will distort new input to protect itself.
Reconstruct
the bowtie
The transform on the retrieval arc. A compressed engram is regrown for the present moment, not returned as a raw core sample. Retrieval is generation.
Narrative Self
the core
Everything circulates around an evolving self-model: not "what I know about the user" but "who I am in this relationship." It updates from every act. Google stores a user profile and has no self at all.

The engine: a real cycle, not gravity

We keep the one thing Google got genuinely right. A background consolidation process still pulls the stream down, strips token noise, and compresses insight into the substrate. That water cycle is a real asset.

But the loop closes. Reconstruction feeds action, action changes the world, the world feeds the stream, and the self re-gates the next pass. One-way sedimentation becomes a closed circuit in which the self both shapes memory and is shaped by it. That bidirectional coupling, the orange spokes at the center, is the whole thesis, and it is exactly what a pile of rock cannot do.
