Stratum 2 · the synthesis spine

How the sources combine into five buildable layers

The center column of the map. The sources do not agree on everything, but they converge on one thesis and split cleanly into five layers, each with a lineage and a verdict.

The thesis they share

Memory is not storage to be retrieved; it is the active process an agent navigates, and is intervened on, to persist across time.

The verb is what unifies them: interpret (Levin), construct (Conway), build identity (Youssef), navigate (NapMem), intervene (Proactive).

1

Hierarchy and goal-filtering

Conway · corroborated by NapMem

Store memory as a partonomy; let current goals gate access. NapMem's pyramid independently re-derives Conway's hierarchy and ships competitively.

2

Active reinterpretation on retrieval

Levin (bowtie) · corroborated by NapMem + Proactive

Retrieval is generation, not lookup. Two measured recipes now exist: NapMem's GRPO-trained navigation and the Proactive agent's selective intervention, whose silence beats always-on injection.

3

Salience and emotional weighting

Damasio + Conway

Emotional significance biases encoding, retrieval, and behavior. Scoring is trivial; wiring it into encoding and behavior is the open work. No measured source covers this yet.

4

Narrative layer and co-emergent self-model

Bruner + Klein/Nichols

The agent itself evolves through the relationship. The Proactive agent's private "status" field is a proto-self-model, but no one has shipped L4 well. Research frontier.

5

Substrate flexibility

Levin (polycomputing)

Memory as an active medium, not a parked value. Still blocked: both measured papers run on ordinary storage and do not dissolve the substrate. The honest unknown.

The live edges

Where the ecosystem is still unresolved

A healthy map shows its open seams, not just its solid ones. These are the questions the sources disagree on or do not yet answer. They are where the next source would attach.

Partially answered

Principles enough, or substrate too?

NapMem and the Proactive agent show the functional layer (hierarchy + reinterpretation + intervention) works on an ordinary substrate. Neither dissolves the substrate, so Levin's no-single-substrate claim stays open.

Open · deployment-relevant

Monolithic navigation vs separate intervention agent

NapMem trains one model to navigate and answer. The Proactive agent keeps the action agent untouched and adds a cheaper sidecar. Different bets, not yet head-to-head. The split is the better default for real deployments.

Open · load-bearing

Is synaptic machinery for interpretation, not storage?

Levin's highest-stakes claim, mostly evidenced in invertebrates. Contradicts mainstream vertebrate neuroscience. Would merit its own dedicated source.

Open · engineering

Narrative coherence has no clean engineering mapping

The fuzziest of the five principles. No published architecture reliably keeps a self-model coherent over long horizons without drift.
